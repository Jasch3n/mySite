Cycle of Life

In a scorching land blowing with blinding dust,
among warm ashes reeking of filthy death,
a bird lay on the ground motionless.

It hears the fire screaming,
the wind howling,
the trees weeping.

It feels the acute pain in itself,
a fiery agony like the flames of hell.
Against the darkness of the night,
it sees from above a brilliant light.

As it rose from the ground,
it heard the fire screaming its apologies,
the wind howling its pity,
the trees weeping its farewell.

Then it understood.

It spanned its wing wide open,
and embraced the fire around it.
It started to burn and shine with a bright flame
that brought not pain but relief.

So it sprung upwards,
like an arrow into the infinite sky,
and became a little shining beacon of light.

May 4, 2021

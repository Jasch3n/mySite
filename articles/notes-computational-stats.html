Notes on Computational Statistics (Brown APMA 1690)

A brief summary of the major results from the course.

Pseudo-random number generation

Pick a prime number $m$ and some number $a$ such that $a\in \{2,\dots,m-1\}$. Pick a seed $R_o \in \{1,\dots m-1\}$. The sequence of pseudo-random numbers is then defined by $R_n = aR_{n-1}\mod m$ To test the "randomness" of our generated sequence, we can utilize the Law of Large Numbers. By this law we have that if $U$ is a uniformly distributed random variable on [0,1], then $\bar U = \frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} h(U_i)$ converges to $E[h(U)]$ for any function $h$. Furthermore, we can run a hypothesis test on the observed value of $\bar U$ and determine if the observation is too unlikely if $U$ was truly uniform [0,1].
